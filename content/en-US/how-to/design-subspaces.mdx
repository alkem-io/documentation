# Design your Subspaces

Subspaces allow you to organise your work into focused projects, themes, or challenges. This guide helps you set up and structure your Subspaces effectively.

## Create a Subspace

Whether or not you are able to create a new Subspace depends on your role and the choices made by the leads of the Space. They can decide whether any member can open a Subspace or if they want to control the set of Subspaces being worked on.

If you have the right privileges, you can find the **Create Button** on the **Subspaces Page**.

You will first be asked some questions to describe the Subspace. Even though you may not know exactly how to solve whatever it is you are working on, you can identify a direction or a few elements that should be taken into account.

Fill in:
- **Title** — a clear, concise name for your Subspace
- **Tagline** — a one-sentence summary of its purpose
- **What** — describe the focus and scope
- **Why** — explain the importance and context
- **Who** — clarify who is involved or interested

You can also choose to use a template for the subspace. Click on the **"FIND TEMPLATE"** button to scroll through the available templates. You can find more information about templates in the templates documentation.

Add **tags** to make your Subspace easier to find for interested stakeholders. This is just a first pass and can (and probably should) always be adjusted and extended as you continue to work on it.

## Set up your Subspace settings

The Subspace has a set of standard settings similar to the Space setup:

- **About** — add visuals, descriptions, tags, and references
- **Community** — manage community roles and members
- **Updates from the Leads** — broadcast messages to members
- **Subspaces** — add and manage sub-Subspaces
- **Settings** — define member permissions and privacy

## Control member access and permissions

In the **Settings** tab, you can:

- Define what members are allowed to do (e.g., create posts, manage content)
- Make this Subspace **private** if needed (non-members are not allowed to view its contents)
- Use privacy controls when you want to restrict access due to privacy issues or when working in a smaller team first

## Organise work into nested Subspaces

Subspaces can have smaller Subspaces within them. Sometimes you need more than one layer to your work, so you can organise the work of a Subspace into smaller Subspaces underneath.

This allows a team of members to:
- Deepen their understanding of specific possibilities
- Identify ideas or directions towards a solution
- Answer key questions like:
  - How can it impact the project?
  - How can we realize it?
  - What resources are needed?

## Use the Innovation Flow

Each Subspace has an **Innovation Flow** — a visual guide that moves the community through the innovation process. The flow visualizes the various steps needed to go from start to end (e.g., Design Thinking or your organization's agreed innovation process).

By moving to the next Innovation Flow state, you communicate progress with your members.

## Create a Coordination Subspace

Many communities create a **private Coordination Subspace** as a sub-community where leads and core team members can:

- Coordinate the next steps
- Plan Posts and content
- Capture meeting notes
- Use brainstorming boards

A template for a Coordination Subspace can be found in the Template Library.
