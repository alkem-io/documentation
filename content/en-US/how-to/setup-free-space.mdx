# Set up your free Space

Ready to create your own Space on Alkemio? This guide walks you through creating a Space and configuring its core settings so you're ready to welcome your community.

## Create your Space

Depending on your account rights, you can create a Space yourself or ask the Alkemio team to set one up for you.

**To create a Space on your own:**
1. Click on your profile picture in the top right corner.
2. Click **My Profile**.
3. Click the gear icon to the right of your name.
4. Go to the **Account** tab.
5. Click the **+** icon in the Hosted Spaces box.
6. Fill in the details of your Space and save.

If you are unable to create a Space yourself, reach out to the Alkemio team — they will set it up for you, after which you can customise it yourself.

## Configure your Space settings

Navigate to your Space settings by clicking the **gear icon** next to the other tabs in your Space. Then click on the **Settings** sub-section. Here you can decide on:

- **Visibility** — public or private
- **Who can join** — any user, invited users only, or users associated with certain organisations
- **Permissions** — what members are allowed to do
- **Application Form Questions** — select questions for people who apply to join

## Set up the other sub-sections

Your Space settings also include the following sub-sections:

- **About** — add a tagline, visuals, What/Why/Who description, tags, and references (links or documents)
- **Layout** — rename the default tabs (Home, Community, Subspaces, Knowledge) to fit your community's language
- **Community** — manage pending applications, invitations, and community roles (Members, Leads, Admins, Host)
- **Updates** — broadcast important messages to all members via the Updates block
- **Subspaces** — add and manage Subspaces directly from here
- **Templates** — decide which templates are available to your community for Posts and Subspaces
- **Storage** — access documents uploaded in your Space

> **Tip:** You don't need to have everything perfect from the start. Begin with the essentials and adjust as your community grows.
