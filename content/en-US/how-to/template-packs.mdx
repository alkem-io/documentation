# Use template packs from other organisations

The Template Library on Alkemio contains template packs contributed by various organisations — from Alkemio itself to knowledge institutes, NGOs, and methodology experts. This guide shows you how to find and use them in your Space.

## What is the Template Library?

The Template Library is a platform-wide collection of whiteboard templates, guided innovation flows, and exercises. It is available to all users and grows continuously as more organisations contribute their best practices.

The library is divided into two sections:

1. **Template Packs** — coherent sets of templates based on a specific theme, methodology, or organisation (e.g. Doughnut Economics, Gamestorming, Novum, Design Think Make Break Repeat)
2. **Browse All Templates** — search by keyword or filter by template type across all available templates

## How to explore the Template Library

1. Click on the **grid icon** (top right of the platform) to open the main menu.
2. Select **Template Library**.
3. Browse the Template Packs or use the search bar to find what you need.
4. Click on a Template Pack to see the individual templates it contains, the contributing organisation, and a description.

## How to use a template from the Library in your Space

**Option 1 — Add it to your Space Template Library:**
1. Go to your Space settings and open the **Templates** sub-section.
2. Click **Library** next to the Collaboration Tool Templates or Subspace Templates section.
3. Browse and select a template to import.
4. The template is now available to all members of your Space when creating Posts or Subspaces.

**Option 2 — Use it directly while creating content:**
1. When creating a Post, Subspace, or Community Guideline, click **"FIND TEMPLATE"**.
2. Scroll past your Space templates to find templates from the General Library.
3. Select the template to apply it immediately.

## Want to contribute your own templates?

Anyone can share templates in the library to support innovation. If you're interested, reach out via the forum or email **info@alkem.io**.
