# Reuse your work through templates

Templates save time, spark ideas for structuring your Space, and help you maintain a consistent experience. Rather than building everything from scratch, you can reuse templates across your own community and draw on templates created by the broader Alkemio community.

## What are templates?

Templates in Alkemio provide a structured way to set up different elements within a Space or Subspace. They help streamline collaboration, standardise processes, and guide community engagement.

You can use templates when creating:
- **Spaces**
- **Subspaces**
- **Collaboration Tools**: Posts, Whiteboards, Collections of Links and Documents, Call for Whiteboards, Call for Posts
- **Community Guidelines**

## Your Space Template Library

Every Space has its own Template Library, which admins manage. Go to the **Templates** sub-section of your Space settings to:

- **Add templates from the General Library**: import existing templates from the platform-wide library to use in your Space
- **Create new templates**: build custom templates tailored to your Space and community

## How to use a template

When creating a Community Guideline, Subspace, or Post, look for the **"FIND TEMPLATE"** button. Clicking it opens a selection of templates:

- Space-specific templates (from your Space Template Library) appear first
- Templates from the General Library appear below

Select a template to pre-fill structured content.

You can also apply a template to just one part of a Post, for example only the whiteboard inside it. Open the whiteboard or collection settings first, then click **"FIND TEMPLATE"** there.

## How to save your work as a template

After creating a Post or Subspace you are happy with, you can save it as a template:

1. Open the three-dot menu (⋮) on the Post or Subspace.
2. Click **Save as Template**.
3. The template will be saved to your Space Template Library.

This way, your team can reuse the same structure across the Space without having to rebuild it each time.
